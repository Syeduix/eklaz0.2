Ask Us Directly →
Do I need a master's degree to apply for a PhD?
In most cases yes, though some integrated MSc/PhD programmes accept strong bachelor's graduates. US PhDs commonly admit straight from undergrad with a master's bundled in.
How long does a PhD take?
UK PhDs: typically 3–4 years full-time. US PhDs: 5–6 years (includes coursework). Part-time and integrated programmes vary.
Can I do a PhD without funding?
Yes, self-funded PhDs are common, but we strongly encourage exploring funding routes first. UK PhD fees for international students are £20,000–£28,000 per year.
What is a research proposal?
A 5–8 page document outlining your research question, why it matters, what's known already, and how you'd investigate it. It's the single most important part of your application.
How do I find a supervisor?
Start by reading recent papers in your field. Identify academics whose published work overlaps with your interests. We help you build that list and write the outreach emails.
